Hardware Implementation of Present Cipher in FPGA

Team Members

Mr. Akshay Omanakuttan (1AT19EC003)

Mr. Arman Ali (1AT19EC010)

Mr. Asutosh Jujare (1AT19EC034)

Miss. Jyoti Kumari (1AT19EC075)

Project Abstract

Our project implements the PRESENT lightweight cipher in FPGA for security applications. We reviewed existing implementations and developed two novel designs that set new records in implementation size and energy consumption. The system is particularly suited for IoT devices in constrained environments.

Key Features

  • Lightweight cipher implementation
  • Optimized for 7nm FinFET technology
  • Energy-efficient architecture
  • High throughput-per-slice

ML-based Acoustic Repellent System for Crop Protection

Team Members

Misba Tabassum (1AT19EC098)

Mohammed Awaiz (1AT19EC100)

Manasa G (1AT19EC094)

Mohammed Irfan G (1AT19EC101)

Project Abstract

This system protects crops from wild animals using machine learning and targeted acoustic frequencies. A CNN model identifies approaching animals through infrared cameras, then emits specific ultrasonic frequencies to repel them without causing physical harm.

Key Features

  • CNN-based animal detection
  • Species-specific frequency generation
  • Infrared night vision capability
  • Raspberry Pi coordination

Communication System for Deaf, Dumb, and Blind

Team Members

Mr. K Rakesh (1AT19EC076)

Mohamed Azeez Afsar (1AT19EC099)

Mohmmed Talib Khasim (1AT19EC102)

Mr. Syed Bilal Ryan (1AT19EC172)

Project Abstract

This innovative system assists individuals with visual, hearing, or vocal impairments through integrated technologies. It combines object recognition, text-to-speech, and speech-to-text conversion in a Raspberry Pi platform to facilitate communication.

Key Features

  • Object recognition for the blind
  • Speech-to-text for the deaf
  • Text-to-speech for the mute
  • Unified Raspberry Pi interface

About

This website showcases the groundbreaking final-year projects developed by students of the Electronics and Communication Department at Atria Institute of Technology. These innovative works span across cutting-edge domains such as IoT, Machine Learning, FPGA, Digital Systems, and Renewable Energy, highlighting the remarkable engineering talent and creativity of our students. Each project is a reflection of not just technical knowledge, but also a deep commitment to solving real-world problems and contributing to societal development. With a strong focus on innovation, functionality, and future scalability, these student-driven initiatives have the potential to shape industries and communities. The students of Atria are not just engineers in the making — they are change-makers and visionaries, pushing boundaries and redefining what’s possible. This platform is a tribute to their dedication, passion, and the exciting future they are building, one project at a time. .
